# The Mission (About, version 2)

Hey Sammy exists to reverse a two-decade decline in showing up. Americans—especially young Americans—spend less time together in person than any generation on record, and Hey Sammy helps bend that line back: one person, one recurring activity, one real place at a time.

## What Happened

Daily in-person socializing for Americans ages 15–24 fell from about 60 minutes in 2003 to 35 minutes in 2025 (American Time Use Survey). Four times more Americans report zero close friends than in 1990 (12% vs. 3%). Young people spend 70% less time at parties than in 2003, date less, drive less, and average 4.8 hours a day on social media—about eight times what they spend socializing in person.

## Why It Matters

The U.S. Surgeon General declared loneliness a public health epidemic in 2023, comparable to smoking up to 15 cigarettes a day, with a 29% increased risk of heart disease, 32% increased risk of stroke, and 50% increased risk of dementia in older adults.

## The Other Side

Strong social relationships are associated with 50% greater odds of survival (Holt-Lunstad meta-analysis of 308,849 people). The Harvard Study of Adult Development found relationship satisfaction at 50 predicts later health better than cholesterol. Hobbies are linked to less depression and more life satisfaction across 16 countries. Friendship is buildable: roughly 50 hours of shared time makes a casual friend, 90 hours a friend, and 200+ hours a close friend (Hall, 2019)—which is why recurring activities beat one-off events.

## Third Places

Third places—run clubs, dance socials, studios, volunteer shifts, rec leagues—bundle everything friendship needs: an activity, a schedule, familiar faces, a skill to learn, and a next week.

## Hey Sammy's Goals

Help every user make one recurring real-world commitment; make showing up alone feel doable; get first-timers back for visits two, three, and four; send committed regulars to local organizers and third places; help people learn real skills; and measure success in regulars, not downloads.

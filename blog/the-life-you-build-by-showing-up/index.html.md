# The Life You Build by Showing Up

Courage - June 18, 2026

Confidence is not the prerequisite. Showing up is how you build a bigger life, one repeated action at a time.

Showing up is the most underrated skill there is.

People talk about confidence like it's a prerequisite - something you need before you start. It's almost always the other way around. Confidence is what you earn after you start. It comes from proving to yourself that you can walk into a room, be new, be awkward, learn something, survive the uncomfortable part, and come back.

> The first win is not being good. The first win is going.

That's never more true than when you're trying to rebuild your social life as an adult. It's so easy to stay home and tell yourself you'll get to it later. Later, when work calms down. Later, when you feel more confident. Later, when you're in better shape. Later, when you know exactly what to do.

But later is slippery.

A week becomes a month. A month becomes a year. Your life gets smaller - not because you chose that, but because you kept postponing the uncomfortable parts of making it bigger.

Hey Sammy exists for that exact moment: the gap between wanting a fuller life and actually walking out the door.

## Action Gives Life A Chance To Surprise You

The magic was never in finding the perfect event. The magic is in action - choosing a real thing, putting it on your calendar, walking through the door, and giving life a chance to surprise you.

Sometimes the surprise is small. You learn the basics of salsa. You survive a beginner pickleball game. You ask someone how long they've been coming. You realize nobody was judging you half as hard as you were judging yourself.

Sometimes it's bigger. You find a hobby you love. You meet people you'd never otherwise have met. You start recognizing faces. You build a weekly ritual. You begin to feel like your city actually belongs to you.

That kind of life is built through tiny, repeated actions. Put on your shoes. Go to the class. Join the run. Ask the question. Come back next week. None of it is dramatic. All of it is powerful.

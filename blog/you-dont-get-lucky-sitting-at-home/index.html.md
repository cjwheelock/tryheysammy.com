# You Don't Get Lucky Sitting at Home

Founder note - June 26, 2026

The simple idea behind Hey Sammy: your life only changes once you put yourself in motion.

I dropped out of college for a year and worked crappy jobs. There was no cinematic version of that year - no traveling the world, finding myself, coming back with perfect clarity. I lived at home. I felt lost. I was sad. I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life.

Here's the thing that year taught me, and I still believe it: you don't wake up one day magically knowing what you want. You find out by trying things. Through elimination, experimentation, and motion. You bump into the real parts of yourself by leaving the house.

> You don't get lucky sitting at home. Action creates results.

Back then I was coaching my old high school track team, and I loved it - the mentoring, the tutoring, the motivating, passing on what I knew. It gave me somewhere to be useful when nothing else was figured out.

Then a friend from the team invited me to learn ballroom dancing in Tucson. Most of my friends spent weekends drinking in someone's garage. No judgment - I was right there with them. But it felt cheap and hollow. We weren't learning anything. We weren't building anything. We were just killing time.

Dancing was the opposite. Social, creative, hard, connective. You learned a skill alongside other people. You had to be bad at something in public and keep going anyway. You had to ask for help and actually let people give it.

## People Help People Who Show Up

When I started dancing, people were kind to me. They taught me for free. Someone handed me a key to the studio. People stayed late to work with me after hours. It changed how I saw the world.

If you show up genuinely - if you're kind, if you ask for help - a lot of people will meet you halfway. Not everyone. But enough. More than you think.

Ballroom led me to West Coast Swing. West Coast Swing led me to people in California who were driven, entrepreneurial, and inspiring - people who made me care more about my own life and imagine a bigger version of it. They're a big part of why I moved from Tucson to San Francisco.

That's what creating your own luck actually looks like. I had no idea dancing would change my life. I just said yes to something random, then kept showing up.

## Why Hey Sammy Exists

Here's what I learned the hard way, and what Hey Sammy is built on: one-off events aren't enough. Real friendships form through familiarity and repetition. See someone once and they're a stranger. See them five times - at pickleball, dance class, a run club, the dog park, a volunteer shift - and suddenly it's easy to say, "Hey, I've seen you here before. What's your name?"

That small moment is everything. Familiarity lowers the stakes. A shared activity gives you something to talk about. Repetition gives trust time to grow.

So Hey Sammy is built around recurring activities, not random events. Run clubs. Dance socials. Pickleball. Pottery. Language exchange. Volunteering. Hiking groups. Places where you do something together, see the same faces, and have a natural reason to talk.

## The Alternative Is Too Easy

The default version of modern life is dangerously passive. Work on a screen. Go home. Scroll TikTok. Watch YouTube. Watch Netflix. Repeat - maybe with some video games or gambling mixed in. A whole year disappears like that. So do five.

I'm not anti-technology. Hey Sammy is an app. But I'm deeply pro-real-world. People are physical, social, three-dimensional creatures. Seeing someone laugh in real life matters. Doing something hard with other people matters. Walking into a room where you know nobody and saying "Hey, I'm new here" matters.

## The First Win Is Showing Up

You don't need to get great at the activity. You don't need to win the tournament, nail the routine, speak the language perfectly, or be the most interesting person in the room. The first win is showing up. The second is talking to one person. The third is coming back.

Courage is almost always smaller than people imagine. Not a grand leap - usually just putting on your shoes, walking through a door, being bad at something, and trying again next week.

That's exactly what Hey Sammy is here for. Sammy is your guide, your planner, and your nudge - getting to know you, tracking the cool things happening near you, and putting you in the path of friendship, skill, confidence, and luck.

If you take one thing from this, take this: waiting rarely changes your life. Action does.

So try one thing this week. Be new. Be awkward. Ask someone how long they've been coming. Show up a second time. Give life a chance to meet you halfway.
